# Case Study: UPI Fraud

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime where scammers trick people into sending money through digital payment apps. It usually happens through fake calls, messages, or links that appear genuine.

In a typical scenario, a scammer calls a student pretending to be from a bank or a company. They may say that the student has won a prize or needs to update their KYC details. The scammer then sends a payment request or a fake link and asks the student to approve it. Without understanding, the victim enters their UPI PIN, which actually authorizes a payment instead of receiving money.

Students and young users are often targeted because they use digital payment apps frequently and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes misuse of personal data.

This type of fraud spreads quickly because scammers use social engineering techniques to create urgency and confusion. Many victims realize the mistake only after the money has been deducted.

Awareness and careful verification are essential to prevent such incidents.
